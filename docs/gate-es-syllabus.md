# GATE Environmental Science & Engineering (ES) — Official Syllabus Reference

> **Authoritative data foundation.** The question-generation model and the
> content team **must** consult this reference before creating any study
> module, mock, or practice question. Every question's `subject` tag should map
> to one of the eight technical sections below (plus **General Aptitude**).
>
> The ES paper was introduced in **GATE 2021** and the section structure has
> been stable since. Unlike most engineering papers, the ES paper does **not**
> carry a separate *Engineering Mathematics* section — it is **General Aptitude
> (15 marks) + 85 marks of subject** spread across the eight sections below.

## Section 1: Environmental Management and Ethics
**Key Topics:** Concepts of sustainability and sustainable development; Carrying
capacity; Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) — methodology, rapid vs.
comprehensive, EMP; Life Cycle Assessment (LCA); Environmental laws &
regulations of India (Water Act 1974, Air Act 1981, Environment Protection Act
1986, hazardous-waste & e-waste rules); Environmental economics (cost–benefit
analysis, polluter-pays, externalities); Environmental ethics; Environmental
auditing and Environmental Management Systems (ISO 14000 series).

**Primary Reference:** *Environmental Engineering & Science* — Davis & Cornwell;
*Introduction to Environmental Engineering & Science* — Masters & Ela.

---

## Section 2: Environmental Chemistry
**Key Topics:** Atmospheric, aquatic and soil chemistry; Chemical equilibria —
acid–base, solubility/precipitation, complexation, redox; Carbonate system,
alkalinity, hardness; pH, pe/Eh; Chemical kinetics (zero/first/second-order);
Photochemistry and photochemical smog chemistry; Nomenclature and properties of
organic pollutants (VOCs, POPs, pesticides); Toxic heavy metals; Units of
expressing concentration (mg/L, ppm, equivalents, mole fraction).

**Primary Reference:** *Environmental Chemistry* — Stanley E. Manahan; *Aquatic
Chemistry* — Stumm & Morgan.

---

## Section 3: Environmental Microbiology
**Key Topics:** Structure and classification of microorganisms; Microbial
metabolism (aerobic/anaerobic, chemotrophs/phototrophs); Microbial growth
kinetics (Monod model, growth curve, yield); Enzyme kinetics
(Michaelis–Menten); Role of microbes in water & wastewater treatment;
Biogeochemical cycles (C, N, S, P); Indicator organisms (coliforms, MPN);
Pathogens and waterborne diseases; Bioassays and toxicity testing.

**Primary Reference:** *Wastewater Engineering: Treatment and Reuse* — Metcalf &
Eddy; *Brock Biology of Microorganisms* — Madigan et al.

---

## Section 4: Ecology and Environmental Biotechnology
**Key Topics:** Ecosystem structure & function; Food chains, food webs, trophic
levels and energy flow (10% law, ecological pyramids); Productivity (GPP/NPP);
Population dynamics (exponential & logistic growth, carrying capacity);
Biodiversity — levels, indices (Shannon, Simpson), hotspots, conservation;
Ecological succession; Bioremediation, phytoremediation, biofuels; Biosensors.

**Primary Reference:** *Fundamentals of Ecology* — Eugene Odum; *Ecology &
Environment* — P.D. Sharma.

---

## Section 5: Air and Noise Pollution
**Key Topics:** Sources and classification of air pollutants (primary &
secondary); Effects on health, materials and vegetation; Meteorology — lapse
rates, atmospheric stability, plume behaviour, mixing height; Gaussian plume
dispersion model; Stack height and effective stack height (plume rise);
Particulate control — gravity settler, cyclone, ESP (Deutsch–Anderson), fabric
filter, wet scrubber; Gaseous control — adsorption, absorption; Ambient & emission
standards (NAAQS); Noise — sound power/pressure/intensity, dB(A) addition,
L_eq, L_dn, noise standards, attenuation and control.

**Primary Reference:** *Air Pollution* — M.N. Rao & H.V.N. Rao; *Environmental
Engineering* — Peavy, Rowe & Tchobanoglous.

---

## Section 6: Water and Wastewater Treatment
**Key Topics:** Water-quality parameters (turbidity, DO, BOD, COD, TOC,
hardness, TDS); Drinking-water standards; Population forecasting & water demand;
Intake, aeration, coagulation–flocculation, sedimentation (overflow rate,
settling velocity, Stokes' law), filtration (rapid sand filter), disinfection
(chlorination, breakpoint, CT concept); Wastewater characteristics; Self-purification
of streams — Streeter–Phelps oxygen-sag, deoxygenation/reaeration; Primary,
secondary (activated sludge — F/M, SRT, MLSS, recycle; trickling filter) and
tertiary treatment; Sludge digestion and disposal; Sewer hydraulics.

**Primary Reference:** *Water Supply & Sanitary Engineering* / *Environmental
Engineering Vol. I & II* — S.K. Garg; *Wastewater Engineering* — Metcalf & Eddy.

---

## Section 7: Solid and Hazardous Waste Management
**Key Topics:** Municipal solid waste — characterisation, generation rate,
physical/chemical composition, calorific value; Collection, transfer and
transport; Processing — size reduction, separation, composting, incineration
(mass/energy balance); Sanitary landfills — siting, design, leachate and
landfill-gas generation; Hazardous waste — characterisation, treatment, secure
landfill; Biomedical and e-waste management; Resource recovery and 3R hierarchy.

**Primary Reference:** *Integrated Solid Waste Management* — Tchobanoglous,
Theisen & Vigil; *Solid Waste Engineering* — Worrell & Vesilind.

---

## Section 8: Global and Regional Environmental Issues
**Key Topics:** Greenhouse effect and global warming; Greenhouse gases and
Global Warming Potential (GWP); Climate change — IPCC, radiative forcing, carbon
footprint and carbon sequestration; Stratospheric ozone depletion (ODP, Montreal
Protocol); Acid rain (formation, effects, control); Eutrophication;
International protocols and conventions (Stockholm, Rio, Kyoto, Paris, Basel,
Ramsar); Transboundary pollution.

**Primary Reference:** *Environmental Science: Earth as a Living Planet* — Botkin
& Keller; *Climate Change* — IPCC Assessment Report summaries.
