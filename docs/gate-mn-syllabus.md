# GATE Mining Engineering (MN) — Official Syllabus Reference

> **Authoritative data foundation.** The question-generation model and the
> content team **must** consult this reference before creating any study
> module, mock, or practice question. Every question's `subject` tag should map
> to one of the six sections below (plus **General Aptitude**).

---

## Section 1: Engineering Mathematics
**Key Topics:** System of linear equations, Eigenvalues/vectors, Cayley–Hamilton
Theorem; Partial derivatives, Maxima/Minima, Taylor's series, Fourier series;
Vector Gradient, Divergence, Curl, Line/Surface/Volume integrals (Stokes, Gauss,
Green's theorems); Linear & Non-linear 1st-order ODEs, Higher-order linear ODEs;
Hypothesis testing, Poisson/Normal/Binomial distributions; Numerical Integration
(Trapezoidal, Simpson's rule).

**Primary Reference:** *Higher Engineering Mathematics* — B.S. Grewal;
*Advanced Engineering Mathematics* — H.K. Dass.

---

## Section 2: Mining Geology, Mine Development & Surveying
**Key Topics:** Minerals/Rocks Classification & Ore Genesis; Structural geology
(folds, faults, dip/strike); Underground drivages & methods of access; Drilling
methods; Explosives & energetics, blasting devices, blast design practices;
Rock-tool interaction; Levels/leveling, tacheometry, triangulation, contouring,
errors/adjustments, underground correlation, curves, photogrammetry, EDM, Total
Station, GPS/GIS.

**Primary Reference:** *Blasting in Metallurgy & Mining* — Pradhan; *Rock
Blasting* — Bhandari; *Advanced Mine Surveying* — S. Ghatak; *Textbook of
Geology* — P.K. Mukerjee.

---

## Section 3: Geomechanics and Ground Control
**Key Topics:** Equations of equilibrium, 2D frames/trusses, Free body diagrams,
Friction, Beam analysis; Geotechnical rock properties, Rock mass classification
(RMR, Q-system), In-situ stress measurement, Rock failure theories, Ground
vibrations, Stress distribution around mine openings, Subsidence, Rock
bursts/coal bumps, Slope stability; Pillar design, Roof support systems, Mine
filling.

**Primary Reference:** *Rock Mechanics for Underground Mining* — Brady & Brown;
*Fundamentals of Rock Mechanics* — Jaeger & Cook.

---

## Section 4: Mining Methods and Machinery
**Key Topics:** Opencast layout, continuous surface mining systems (bucket wheel
excavators, surface miners), Highwall mining; Bord & Pillar, Room & Pillar,
Longwall mining, Thick seam methods; Open, supported, and caved stoping methods,
stope mechanization, ore handling; Power transmission (mechanical, hydraulic,
pneumatic), Material handling (haulages, conveyors, face machinery, hoisting
systems, pumps, crushers).

**Primary Reference:** *Surface Mining* — G.B. Misra; *Principles & Practices of
Modern Coal Mining* — R.D. Singh; *Elements of Mining Technology* (Vols I, II,
III) — D.J. Deshmukh.

---

## Section 5: Surface Environment, Mine Ventilation & Underground Hazards
**Key Topics:** Air, water, soil pollution standards, dispersion, noise, land
reclamation; Underground atmosphere, Heat sources, Thermal environment/cooling;
Airflow mechanics, natural/mechanical ventilation, Mine fans, Auxiliary
ventilation, Ventilation networks (Kirchhoff's laws); Mine gases, Methane
drainage, Fires, Explosions, Dust, Inundation hazards, Rescue apparatus, Safety
management, Mine lighting, Mine legislation, DGMS Coal/Metal Regulations, Act &
Rules.

**Primary Reference:** *Mine Ventilation and Air Conditioning* — Hartman &
Mutmansky; *Mine Environment & Ventilation* — G.B. Misra.

---

## Section 6: Mineral Economics, Mine Planning & Systems Engineering
**Key Topics:** Mineral resource classification (UNFC), DCF analysis, NPV, IRR,
Mine valuation, taxation; Sampling methods, Reserve estimation, Geostatistics,
Facility location optimization, Mine life/size, Ultimate pit configuration,
Optimum mill cut-off grade; System reliability (series, parallel, active
standby), Maintainability/Availability, Linear Programming (simplex,
transportation, assignment), Network analysis (CPM/PERT), Inventory, Queuing
theory, Decision trees.

**Primary Reference:** *Mineral Processing Technology* — Wills & Finch; *Mine
Economics* — S.C. Ray; *Operations Research* — Kanti Swarup.
