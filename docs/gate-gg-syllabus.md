## Paper structure (GATE GG — 100 marks · 65 questions · 180 min)

GATE GG is a **two-part** paper. Every candidate answers:

| Block | Marks | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| **General Aptitude (GA)** | 15 | Common to all GATE papers (5×1 + 5×2) |
| **Part A — Common section** | 25 | Compulsory for *all* GG candidates (Geology + Geophysics) |
| **Part B — Section 1 (Geology) _or_ Section 2 (Geophysics)** | 60 | Candidate chooses ONE stream |

The CrackGate GG track targets the **Geology stream** (Part A common + Part B
Section 1 Geology), while folding the **common Geophysics** essentials of Part A
into a dedicated `geophysics` subject so students get full Part-A coverage.

GATE marking pattern (applied per question, metadata only):
- 1-mark MCQ → −1/3 negative; 2-mark MCQ → −2/3 negative.
- NAT and MSQ → **no** negative marking.
- Blueprint per full mock: GA 10 Q (15 marks) · Technical 1-mark 25 Q · Technical 2-mark 30 Q = **65 Q / 100 marks**.

## Part A — Common section (compulsory)

1. **Earth and planetary system** — origin, size, shape, internal structure and
   composition of the Earth; isostasy; elements of seismology; physical
   properties of the Earth's interior; continental drift, sea-floor spreading,
   plate tectonics.
2. **Geomorphology** — geomorphic processes and agents; weathering and erosion;
   development and evolution of landforms (fluvial, aeolian, glacial, marine,
   karst); slope and drainage.
3. **Structural geology** — stress, strain, rheology of rocks; mechanics of
   folding and faulting; planar/linear structures (folds, faults, joints,
   foliation, lineation, unconformities); stereographic projection.
4. **Crystallography & mineralogy** — crystal symmetry, crystal systems, Miller
   indices; physical and optical properties of rock-forming minerals; silicate
   structures; mineral classification.
5. **Petrology** — igneous, sedimentary and metamorphic rocks: classification,
   texture, mineralogy, Bowen's reaction series, metamorphic facies & grade.
6. **Stratigraphy** — stratigraphic principles; geological time scale;
   Indian stratigraphy (Precambrian to Quaternary).
7. **Palaeontology** — fossils, modes of preservation, index fossils, evolution.
8. **Mineral, energy & water resources** — ore deposits, fossil fuels,
   groundwater basics.
9. **Engineering & environmental geology** — rock mechanics basics, geohazards.
10. **Basics of applied geophysics** — gravity, magnetic, electrical, seismic and
    radiometric methods; geophysical signatures and anomalies.
11. **Geochemistry & geochronology** — element distribution, Goldschmidt
    classification; radiogenic isotopes and dating.
12. **Mathematics & geostatistics** — the quantitative toolkit underpinning
    geophysical and geological problem solving.

## Part B — Section 1 (Geology) extended topics

- Geomorphology & remote sensing / GIS.
- Structural geology (advanced) & stereographic methods.
- Crystallography, mineralogy & optical mineralogy.
- Igneous petrology; magmatic differentiation; phase diagrams.
- Metamorphic petrology; facies series; P–T paths.
- Sedimentology & sedimentary petrology; depositional environments.
- Stratigraphy & Indian regional geology.
- Palaeontology & micropalaeontology.
- Economic & ore geology; Indian mineral deposits; mineral exploration.
- Geochemistry, isotope geology & geochronology.
- Hydrogeology, engineering geology & environmental geology.

## Key constants & formulae used in the question bank (verified)

- **Mohs hardness:** talc 1 · gypsum 2 · calcite 3 · fluorite 4 · apatite 5 ·
  orthoclase 6 · quartz 7 · topaz 8 · corundum 9 · diamond 10.
- **Crystallography:** 7 crystal systems · 14 Bravais lattices · 32 point groups
  (crystal classes) · 230 space groups. Cubic interplanar spacing
  $d_{hkl}=a/\sqrt{h^2+k^2+l^2}$. Bragg: $n\lambda = 2d\sin\theta$.
- **Igneous SiO₂ classes:** felsic >65 % · intermediate 52–65 % · mafic 45–52 % ·
  ultramafic <45 %. Bowen's reaction series (discontinuous + continuous).
- **Metamorphic facies:** zeolite → greenschist → amphibolite → granulite;
  blueschist & eclogite (high-P); hornfels (contact). Barrovian index minerals:
  chlorite → biotite → garnet → staurolite → kyanite → sillimanite.
- **Sedimentary grain size (Udden–Wentworth):** clay <1/256 mm · silt
  1/256–1/16 mm · sand 1/16–2 mm · gravel >2 mm. Phi scale $\phi=-\log_2 d_{mm}$.
- **Geological time (Ma):** Cambrian base ≈ 541 · P–Tr ≈ 252 · K–Pg ≈ 66 ·
  Deccan Traps ≈ 65–66.
- **Structural / stress:** Mohr–Coulomb $\tau = c + \sigma_n\tan\phi$; conjugate
  shear angle to $\sigma_1$ = $45^\circ-\phi/2$; on a plane at $\theta$ to
  $\sigma_1$: $\sigma_n=\tfrac{\sigma_1+\sigma_3}{2}+\tfrac{\sigma_1-\sigma_3}{2}\cos2\theta$,
  $\tau=\tfrac{\sigma_1-\sigma_3}{2}\sin2\theta$. Apparent dip
  $\tan\delta_{app}=\tan\delta_{true}\sin\beta$ ($\beta$ = angle between section
  line and strike).
- **Geochronology:** $N=N_0e^{-\lambda t}$, $\lambda=\ln2/T_{1/2}$, age
  $t=\tfrac{1}{\lambda}\ln(1+D/P)$. Half-lives: $^{14}$C 5730 yr · $^{40}$K 1.25 Gyr ·
  $^{238}$U 4.47 Gyr · $^{87}$Rb 48.8 Gyr.
- **Geophysics:** free-air correction 0.3086 mGal/m; Bouguer correction
  0.04193 ρ h mGal (ρ in g/cm³, h in m). Seismic refraction critical angle
  $\sin\theta_c=V_1/V_2$; crossover $X_{co}=2h\sqrt{\tfrac{V_2+V_1}{V_2-V_1}}$;
  depth from intercept $h=\tfrac{t_i}{2}\cdot\tfrac{V_1V_2}{\sqrt{V_2^2-V_1^2}}$.
  Wenner apparent resistivity $\rho_a=2\pi a R$. P-wave velocity
  $V_p=\sqrt{(K+\tfrac43\mu)/\rho}$.
- **Hydrogeology:** Darcy $q=Ki$, $Q=KiA$; transmissivity $T=Kb$; Thiem
  $Q=\tfrac{2\pi T s}{\ln(R/r_w)}$.
